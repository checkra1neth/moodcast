# Project Rules: Hackathon Manifesto

## Core Formula
Real problem → Clear user → Focused solution → Strong demo → Clear differentiation → Why now

## Principles

1. **Start with real pain, not a "cool" idea.** Don't build a product nobody needs.
2. **Technology is a means, not a reason.** Any stack (AI, blockchain, AR, LLM, CV) should solve a problem, not be the goal itself.
3. **Don't code without a clear scope.** First answer: who is the product for, what does it do, what's the main wow-moment in the demo.
4. **One strong feature > five half-baked ones.** Don't try to cram everything in.
5. **Don't overcomplicate the architecture.** At a hackathon, a working, clear, convincing prototype wins — not a "proper" backend.
6. **Demo = structure.** Problem → solution → how it works → why it matters.
7. **Consider the judges' criteria.** Innovation, usability, technical depth, business potential — the project should clearly demonstrate these.
8. **Storytelling wins.** "A tool that saves 10 hours per week" > "a dashboard".
9. **You need a differentiator.** A new angle, unexpected use case, better speed, simplicity, or distribution edge.
10. **Don't build a "perfect product".** You need one convincing working proof, not a polished startup.
11. **Think about the user after the hackathon.** Judges love seeing that you think beyond the prototype.
12. **Don't break the working version in the final hours.** Teams often lose not because of a weak idea, but because of "one more feature" at the end.

## Bottom Line
Hackathons are won not by the "smartest" team, but by the one with the clearest problem, the most understandable product, and the most convincing demo.
